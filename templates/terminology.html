ATM: Automated Teller Machine. Places where you can withdraw cash without having to visit a bank's physical branches.



Bank: A business used to protect money for its consumers. Banks are intermediates between those loaning money and those borrowing money to and from the institution respectively.



Cashback: A reward system that enables the consumer to earn a percentage of what they spend back when shopping.



Child Account: Just like a regular current or savings account, however often with restrictions and/or parental control. They aim to help children understand money management and can be used for higher education savings.



Credit Card: A card that is provided by your bank enabling the instantaneous borrowing of money up to an amount. This is provided with the assurance that you will repay what you owe at a future date. The repayment normally includes interest.



Current Account: An account to store and withdraw your money. This account lets you manage your day-to-day spending. Banks often offer a variety of different current accounts all offering a slightly different product.



ESG Rating: Environmental, Social and Governance rating. A rating designed to measure a company's focus and direction in regard to environmental, social and governance risks currently present in our lives.



Existing Customer Savings: In order to open up this type of savings account you must already hold an account with that bank.



Interest: Money that is paid for the use of money lent or for a delayed repayment. The rate can be fixed or variable, however will be stated before entering the agreement.



ISA: An Individual Savings Account. This is like a savings account however offers tax-free interest payments. There is an annual restriction on the amount of money one can invest that year.



Joint Account: An account where more than one person is responsible for the finances within it.



Mortgages: A loan used to help you buy or manage real estate. The loan is secured by the property itself meaning a fail of payment means the lender can take the real estate. The loans come with interest rates to be repaid.



Open to All Savings: Anyone is able to open a savings account even if you do not already hold an account with the bank.



Overdraft: An overdraft occurs when you withdraw more money than is present in your bank account. It is a form of debt and is repayable on demand. Banks offer a certain amount that your account can be overdrawn.



Packages: A packaged account that comes as a normal bank account along with added extras. These extras might consist of travel insurance and loans.



Prepaid Accounts: These are accounts for children where parents are able to add to and monitor the account. The assurance of this card is once no money is in the account, no money can be spent. They aim to teach children about banking in safety before they open an account of their own.



Premium Bank Accounts: These accounts require a monthly fee in return for better services. These services might consist of retail discounts and breakdown cover. Similar to packaged bank accounts.



Retail Bank: A bank that offers services to the general public, including individuals and small businesses. Retail banks offer services such as current accounts, savings accounts, loans, mortgages, etc.
